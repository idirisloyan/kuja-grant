**Subject:** Proximate Fund — direct funding for Sudanese community
groups (please circulate)

Dear [name / colleagues],

Adeso has launched the **Proximate Fund**, a pooled facility that
funds Sudanese community groups **directly** — using community
endorsement in place of formal registration requirements.

We would be grateful if you could circulate this to local responders
in your network. The essentials:

- **Who it's for:** informal community groups actively serving their
  localities (cash distribution, food security, WASH, protection of
  displaced households). Formal NGO registration is **not** required.
- **How it works:** a group nominates itself through a short
  five-question form (Arabic-first, phone-friendly, ~5 minutes).
  Local elders and coordinators then vouch for the group through the
  same zero-login system. Adeso screens every partner against UN, EU,
  US and World Bank sanctions lists and contacts them within days.
- **How money moves:** small, tranche-based disbursements through
  bank, mobile money, or hawala, with community-verified reporting
  after each disbursement — designed for the realities of operating
  in Sudan today.
- **Nomination link:**
  https://web-production-6f8a.up.railway.app/proximate-nominate
- A printable Arabic/English one-pager with a QR code is attached —
  it works well on notice boards and in WhatsApp groups.

We never ask for bank details through the public link; financial
verification happens later, directly with Adeso staff.

Happy to brief your team or answer questions — and if your members
have groups they would vouch for, we would welcome an introduction.
