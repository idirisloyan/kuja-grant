PROJECT PROPOSAL

TITLE: Strengthening Maternal and Child Health Services in Wajir County, Kenya

SUBMITTED BY: Amani Community Development
DATE: January 10, 2026
DONOR: Women's Protection and Empowerment Fund
REQUESTED AMOUNT: $350,000 (USD)
PROJECT DURATION: 24 months (April 2026 - March 2028)

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Amani Community Development proposes a 24-month project to strengthen maternal and child health (MCH) services in Wajir County, northeastern Kenya. The project will train 50 community health workers, equip 10 health facilities, and establish community-based surveillance systems to reduce maternal and infant mortality in an underserved region where 68% of births occur without skilled attendance.

2. PROBLEM STATEMENT
Wajir County has some of the poorest health indicators in Kenya:
- Maternal mortality ratio: 689 per 100,000 live births (national average: 362)
- Under-5 mortality rate: 82 per 1,000 (national average: 43)
- Skilled birth attendance: 32% (national average: 62%)
- Antenatal care (4+ visits): 18% (national average: 58%)

Key barriers include: limited health infrastructure, shortage of skilled health workers, cultural barriers to facility-based delivery, long distances to health facilities, and limited health education.

3. PROJECT OBJECTIVES
Goal: Reduce maternal and infant mortality in target communities by 30% over 24 months.

Objective 1: Increase skilled birth attendance from 32% to 55% in target communities
Objective 2: Improve antenatal care coverage (4+ visits) from 18% to 45%
Objective 3: Establish functional community health surveillance in 20 villages
Objective 4: Strengthen capacity of 10 health facilities for emergency obstetric care

4. METHODOLOGY
4.1 Community Health Worker Training
- Recruit and train 50 CHWs (30 female, 20 male) on MCH, referral protocols, and community mobilization
- Equip CHWs with basic medical kits, mobile phones, and transport allowances
- Establish monthly supervision and mentoring system

4.2 Health Facility Strengthening
- Assess and upgrade 10 health facilities with basic emergency obstetric care equipment
- Train 30 health workers on emergency obstetric and newborn care (EmONC)
- Establish medicine and supply chain systems

4.3 Community Engagement
- Conduct community dialogue sessions (4 per village, 20 villages)
- Establish birth preparedness committees in each village
- Engage religious and cultural leaders as health champions

4.4 Health Information Systems
- Deploy mobile-based reporting system for CHWs
- Establish community-based surveillance for maternal and newborn deaths
- Conduct quarterly facility data quality assessments
