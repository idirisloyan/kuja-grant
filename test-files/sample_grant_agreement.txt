GRANT AGREEMENT

USAID EAST AFRICA WASH PROGRAM 2026-2028
Grant Reference: USAID-EA-WASH-2026-001

BETWEEN:
United States Agency for International Development (USAID)
Through: Global Health Fund (Implementing Partner)

AND:
[Implementing Organization Name]

1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW

1.1 The USAID East Africa WASH Program aims to improve access to safe water, sanitation, and hygiene services in underserved communities across Kenya, Somalia, and Uganda. The program will target rural and peri-urban communities with populations between 5,000 and 50,000 people.

1.2 Total Grant Amount: USD 2,500,000 (Two Million Five Hundred Thousand US Dollars)
1.3 Program Duration: 24 months from the date of signing
1.4 Expected Start Date: July 1, 2026
1.5 Expected End Date: June 30, 2028

2. ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

2.1 The implementing organization must:
a) Be a registered non-governmental organization in at least one of the target countries (Kenya, Somalia, or Uganda)
b) Have a minimum of 3 years experience implementing WASH programs
c) Demonstrate an annual operating budget of at least USD 200,000
d) Have valid organizational registration with the relevant government authority
e) Maintain a Protection from Sexual Exploitation and Abuse (PSEA) policy
f) Have undergone an independent financial audit within the past 2 years

3. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Financial Reporting
The grantee shall submit quarterly financial reports within 30 days of the end of each quarter. Each financial report must include:
- Detailed expenditure statement against approved budget lines
- Variance analysis with explanation for deviations exceeding 10%
- Bank reconciliation statement
- Updated cash flow projection for the next quarter
- Supporting documentation for expenditures above USD 5,000

3.2 Narrative Progress Reports
Semi-annual narrative progress reports are due within 45 days of the end of each six-month period. Each report must include:
- Executive summary of activities completed
- Progress against approved workplan milestones
- Beneficiary data disaggregated by gender and age
- Challenges encountered and mitigation measures
- Updated risk register
- Photos and case studies demonstrating impact

3.3 Annual Impact Assessment
An annual impact assessment report is due within 60 days of each program anniversary. This report must include:
- Baseline comparison data
- Key performance indicator progress against targets
- Independent verification of reported results where possible
- Lessons learned and best practices
- Sustainability assessment

3.4 Final Completion Report
A comprehensive final completion report is due within 90 days of program end. This report must cover:
- Complete narrative of all program activities and achievements
- Final financial report with audited statements
- Asset disposition plan
- Sustainability and exit strategy
- Recommendations for future programming

4. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS

4.1 The following KPIs will be tracked throughout the program:
a) Number of people with improved access to safe water (Target: 150,000)
b) Number of sanitation facilities constructed or rehabilitated (Target: 500)
c) Number of community health workers trained in WASH practices (Target: 200)
d) Reduction in waterborne disease incidence in target communities (Target: 40%)
e) Budget utilization rate (Target: 85-95%)
f) Number of communities with functional water management committees (Target: 50)

5. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

5.1 The grantee must comply with:
a) USAID Standard Provisions for Non-US NGOs
b) All applicable local laws and regulations in the target countries
c) Environmental Impact Assessment requirements for infrastructure projects
d) USAID Branding and Marking requirements
e) Fly America Act requirements for international travel
f) Prohibition on support to terrorism (Executive Order 13224)

5.2 The grantee must maintain adequate records for a period of 3 years following the end of the grant period and make them available for audit upon request.

6. PAYMENT SCHEDULE

6.1 Funding will be disbursed as follows:
- Initial advance: 25% of total grant upon signing (USD 625,000)
- Quarterly disbursements: Based on approved financial reports and workplan progress
- Final payment: 10% retention released upon acceptance of final completion report

7. AMENDMENTS AND MODIFICATIONS

7.1 Any modification to this agreement exceeding 10% budget reallocation between line items requires prior written approval from USAID through the Global Health Fund.

7.2 No-cost extensions may be requested no later than 90 days before the end of the agreement period.
