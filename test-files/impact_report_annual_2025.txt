AMANI COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT
ANNUAL IMPACT REPORT 2025

COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKERS SCALE-UP PROGRAM
Grant: GHF-2025-0147
Reporting Period: January 1 - December 31, 2025

SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
In 2025, the Community Health Workers Scale-Up Program made significant progress toward improving health outcomes in target communities across Garissa and Wajir counties. Key achievements include training and deploying 25 community health workers, reaching 12,450 community members with health education, and facilitating 3,200 health facility referrals.

SECTION 2: KEY ACHIEVEMENTS

2.1 Community Health Worker Deployment
- 25 CHWs trained and deployed across 15 villages (target: 25 - 100% achieved)
- 22 CHWs remained active throughout the year (88% retention rate)
- CHWs conducted 15,600 household visits over the year
- Average 52 visits per CHW per month

2.2 Health Education and Outreach
- 12,450 community members reached with health education (target: 10,000 - 124.5% achieved)
- 186 community health education sessions conducted
- 45 community dialogue events on maternal health
- 2,340 women enrolled in antenatal care through CHW referrals

2.3 Health Facility Referrals
- 3,200 total referrals facilitated (target: 2,500 - 128% achieved)
- 890 maternal health referrals
- 1,450 child health referrals (immunization, malnutrition, illness)
- 860 general health referrals
- 78% referral completion rate

2.4 Capacity Building
- 6 training workshops conducted for CHWs
- 3 refresher training sessions
- 12 monthly supervision visits completed
- 2 exchange visits with other CHW programs

SECTION 5: CHALLENGES AND LESSONS LEARNED

5.1 Challenges
1. Access constraints due to seasonal flooding in Garissa County during April-May, disrupting activities for 6 weeks
2. CHW attrition: 3 CHWs left the program due to migration, requiring replacement and retraining
3. Vaccine cold chain challenges at two health facilities limited immunization coverage
4. Cultural barriers to skilled birth attendance in remote pastoralist communities

5.2 Lessons Learned
1. Engaging religious leaders early significantly improved community acceptance
2. Mobile phone-based reporting improved data quality and timeliness
3. Female CHWs had higher acceptance rates for maternal health messaging
4. Community health committees strengthened local ownership and sustainability
