================================================================================
RISK SCORING METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

LIKELIHOOD SCALE:
1 = Very Unlikely  (less than 10% probability)
2 = Unlikely       (10-30% probability)
3 = Possible       (30-60% probability)
4 = Likely         (60-80% probability)
5 = Very Likely    (greater than 80% probability)

IMPACT SCALE:
1 = Negligible     (minimal effect on program delivery)
2 = Minor          (slight delay or cost increase, easily managed)
3 = Moderate       (significant delay or cost increase, requires management action)
4 = Major          (severe impact on program objectives, requires donor notification)
5 = Critical       (program failure, loss of life, or major reputational damage)

RISK RATING = Likelihood x Impact
1-4   = LOW (Green)      - Accept and monitor
5-9   = MEDIUM (Yellow)  - Active management required
10-15 = HIGH (Orange)    - Senior management attention, enhanced mitigation
16-25 = CRITICAL (Red)   - Immediate action required, donor notification

CATEGORY A: SECURITY RISKS
================================================================================

RISK #1: Armed Conflict and Access Denial
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Security
Description:    Escalation of armed conflict between Al-Shabaab, clan militias,
                and government forces in Lower Jubba results in denial of
                access to target communities, suspension of program activities,
                and potential harm to staff.
Likelihood:     4 (Likely)
Impact:         5 (Critical)
Risk Rating:    20 - CRITICAL (Red)
Risk Owner:     Security Advisor

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Maintain active engagement with INSO for real-time security briefings and
   threat analysis. Attend weekly security coordination meetings.
2. Develop and regularly test (quarterly) hibernation and evacuation plans for
   all operational locations.
3. Implement a remote management protocol that enables continued program
   oversight through local partner networks when direct access is denied.
4. Pre-position 3 months of program supplies in community-managed warehouses
   in each target district.
5. Maintain a 48-hour communication protocol with all field staff through
   satellite phones and VHF radio network.
6. Conduct monthly security context analysis and adjust operational plans.

Contingency Plan: Activate remote management modality; redirect activities to
accessible areas; engage local partners under existing MOUs for continuity.

Residual Risk Rating: 12 - HIGH (Orange)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #2: Targeted Attack on Staff or Assets
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Security
Description:    Direct targeting of JVDA staff, vehicles, or office/warehouse
                facilities by armed groups through IED, ambush, armed robbery,
                or kidnapping.
Likelihood:     3 (Possible)
Impact:         5 (Critical)
Risk Rating:    15 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Security Advisor

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Enforce strict movement protocols: no travel after 4pm, mandatory convoy
   requirements, pre-approved routes only, GPS tracking on all vehicles.
2. Maintain low organizational visibility: unmarked vehicles, discreet office
   locations, minimal external branding in high-risk areas.
3. All staff complete Security Risk Management training (SRM Level 1) within
   first month of employment; field managers complete SRM Level 2.
4. Implement Hostile Environment Awareness Training (HEAT) for all field staff.
5. Maintain kidnap and ransom insurance and emergency response retainer with
   specialist crisis management firm.
6. Daily security check-in protocol for all staff in field locations.

Contingency Plan: Activate incident management team; engage crisis management
firm; notify donor and authorities within 2 hours; implement family liaison.

Residual Risk Rating: 10 - HIGH (Orange)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #3: Community-Level Tensions and Clan Conflict
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Security / Programmatic
Description:    Targeting of specific communities or beneficiary selection
                perceived as biased triggers inter-clan tensions, potentially
                leading to violence at distribution points or program sites.
Likelihood:     3 (Possible)
Impact:         4 (Major)
Risk Rating:    12 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Program Manager

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Apply transparent, community-validated beneficiary selection criteria using
   participatory methods involving all clan representatives.
2. Engage clan elders and local authorities in program design and monitoring.
3. Ensure distribution sites are in neutral locations agreed upon by all parties.
4. Train all field staff in conflict sensitivity and Do No Harm principles.
5. Conduct regular conflict analysis using the Do No Harm framework to identify
   dividers and connectors in target communities.
6. Maintain separate complaints channels for sensitive clan-related grievances.

Contingency Plan: Suspend activities in affected area; convene mediation
dialogue with clan elders; revise beneficiary targeting if bias identified.

Residual Risk Rating: 6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)


CATEGORY B: OPERATIONAL RISKS
================================================================================

RISK #4: Supply Chain Disruption
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Operational
Description:    Disruption to the supply chain for food commodities, agricultural
                inputs, or NFIs due to port congestion in Mogadishu/Kismayo,
                road blockages, or global supply shortages.
Likelihood:     4 (Likely)
Impact:         3 (Moderate)
Risk Rating:    12 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Logistics Officer

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Maintain minimum 2-month buffer stock at regional warehouse in Kismayo.
2. Develop relationships with minimum 3 qualified suppliers per commodity type.
3. Include supply chain contingency clauses in all vendor contracts.
4. Shift to cash and voucher assistance (CVA) as primary modality where markets
   are functional, reducing dependence on physical commodity supply chain.
5. Monitor Logistics Cluster alerts and FEWS NET market bulletins weekly.
6. Pre-negotiate framework agreements with suppliers for rapid procurement.

Contingency Plan: Activate alternative supply routes via Mombasa; switch to
CVA modality; request pipeline support from WFP.

Residual Risk Rating: 6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #5: Staff Turnover and Capacity Gaps
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Operational
Description:    Loss of key technical staff to competitors offering higher
                salaries, or difficulty recruiting qualified specialists for
                remote field locations, leading to implementation delays.
Likelihood:     3 (Possible)
Impact:         3 (Moderate)
Risk Rating:    9 - MEDIUM (Yellow)
Risk Owner:     HR Manager

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Conduct annual salary benchmarking using NGO Consortium survey data and
   adjust compensation to remain competitive (target: 75th percentile).
2. Implement retention bonuses for staff completing 12-month assignments in
   hardship locations.
3. Cross-train staff across functions to reduce single-point-of-failure risks.
4. Maintain a talent pipeline through university partnerships and intern programs.
5. Invest in professional development and clear career progression pathways.
6. Conduct quarterly staff satisfaction surveys and address concerns promptly.

Contingency Plan: Deploy surge roster staff from HQ; engage short-term
consultants for critical technical gaps; redistribute workload across team.

Residual Risk Rating: 6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #6: Vehicle Accident or Mechanical Failure
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Operational / Safety
Description:    Vehicle accident on unpaved roads during field operations
                causing injury to staff or damage to assets, or mechanical
                breakdown stranding staff in insecure areas.
Likelihood:     3 (Possible)
Impact:         4 (Major)
Risk Rating:    12 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Logistics Officer

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Enforce strict vehicle management policy: speed limits (60km/h paved,
   40km/h unpaved), mandatory seatbelts, no night driving.
2. All drivers must hold valid licenses, complete defensive driving training,
   and pass annual competency assessments.
3. Monthly vehicle inspections and preventive maintenance schedules.
4. Carry emergency repair kits, first aid kits, and satellite communication
   equipment in all vehicles.
5. Implement vehicle tracking system with real-time monitoring.
6. Maintain comprehensive vehicle insurance with medical evacuation coverage.

Contingency Plan: Activate medical evacuation plan; deploy recovery vehicle;
conduct accident investigation; report to donor within 24 hours.

Residual Risk Rating: 6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)


CATEGORY C: FINANCIAL RISKS
================================================================================

RISK #7: Currency Fluctuation and Exchange Rate Loss
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Financial
Description:    Significant depreciation of the Somali Shilling against USD
                erodes purchasing power of local currency denominated costs,
                or unfavorable exchange rate conversions reduce available funds.
Likelihood:     4 (Likely)
Impact:         3 (Moderate)
Risk Rating:    12 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Finance Director

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Maintain donor funds in USD accounts; convert to local currency only for
   immediate payment needs (maximum 2-week operational float).
2. Include 3% exchange rate contingency line in all budgets.
3. Monitor exchange rate trends weekly using Central Bank of Somalia and
   hawala market rates.
4. Negotiate fixed-rate contracts with major vendors where possible.
5. Use UN operational exchange rate for all financial reporting.
6. Request budget amendments if cumulative exchange losses exceed 5%.

Contingency Plan: Apply for budget reallocation from contingency line; request
additional funding or no-cost extension from donor.

Residual Risk Rating: 6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #8: Fraud or Financial Mismanagement
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Financial / Reputational
Description:    Internal fraud, misappropriation of funds, or financial
                mismanagement by staff or partners leading to financial loss,
                donor suspension, and reputational damage.
Likelihood:     2 (Unlikely)
Impact:         5 (Critical)
Risk Rating:    10 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Finance Director

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Implement robust segregation of duties: no single person controls an entire
   financial transaction from initiation to payment.
2. Dual authorization required for all bank transactions above $500.
3. Monthly bank reconciliations reviewed by Finance Director.
4. Quarterly internal audits conducted by independent internal auditor.
5. Annual external audit by internationally recognized audit firm.
6. Mandatory fraud awareness training for all staff (annual).
7. Active whistleblower mechanism with protection guarantees.
8. Vendor screening and due diligence for all procurements above $1,000.

Contingency Plan: Suspend implicated staff; engage forensic auditor; notify
donor and authorities within 24 hours; implement recovery actions.

Residual Risk Rating: 5 - MEDIUM (Yellow)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #9: Funding Delays or Reduction
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Financial
Description:    Delays in donor disbursements or unexpected funding cuts result
                in cash flow shortages, forcing suspension of activities or
                delayed payments to staff and vendors.
Likelihood:     3 (Possible)
Impact:         4 (Major)
Risk Rating:    12 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Finance Director

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Maintain organizational cash reserve equivalent to 2 months of operating
   costs ($60,000 minimum).
2. Submit timely and high-quality financial reports to minimize donor
   processing delays.
3. Maintain proactive communication with donor program officers regarding
   disbursement timelines and any anticipated delays.
4. Diversify funding sources to avoid over-reliance on a single donor.
5. Negotiate advance payment terms with key vendors.
6. Develop a phased activity plan that can be scaled based on available funding.

Contingency Plan: Prioritize life-saving activities; negotiate accelerated
disbursement with donor; scale back non-critical activities.

Residual Risk Rating: 6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)


CATEGORY D: REPUTATIONAL RISKS
================================================================================

RISK #10: Safeguarding Incident (SEA/SH)
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Reputational / Protection
Description:    An incident of sexual exploitation and abuse (SEA) or sexual
                harassment (SH) involving JVDA staff, partners, or contractors
                causes harm to beneficiaries and severe reputational damage.
Likelihood:     2 (Unlikely)
Impact:         5 (Critical)
Risk Rating:    10 - HIGH (Orange)
Risk Owner:     Executive Director

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Comprehensive PSEA policy implemented and communicated to all staff,
   partners, and contractors (signed acknowledgment required).
2. Background checks and reference checks for all new hires, including
   participation in the Inter-Agency Misconduct Disclosure Scheme.
3. Two trained safeguarding focal points (1 male, 1 female) at each
   operational location.
4. Annual mandatory PSEA training for all staff and community volunteers.
5. Multiple reporting channels including confidential hotline.
6. Code of Conduct signed by all staff, with clear consequences for violations.
7. Regular community awareness sessions on beneficiary rights and reporting.

Contingency Plan: Activate investigation protocol within 24 hours; provide
survivor support; notify donor within 48 hours; engage external investigator
for serious allegations; take disciplinary action as warranted.

Residual Risk Rating: 5 - MEDIUM (Yellow)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #11: Negative Media Coverage or Public Perception
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Reputational
Description:    Negative media reporting (social media, traditional media)
                regarding program quality, beneficiary targeting, or
                organizational conduct damages JVDA's reputation and
                relationships with stakeholders.
Likelihood:     2 (Unlikely)
Impact:         3 (Moderate)
Risk Rating:    6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)
Risk Owner:     Communications Officer

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Proactive communications strategy with regular updates on program
   achievements shared with media and stakeholders.
2. Social media monitoring for mentions of JVDA and program activities.
3. Transparent community communication about program objectives, beneficiary
   selection criteria, and available resources.
4. Spokesperson training for senior management.
5. Pre-approved media response protocols for sensitive issues.
6. Strong community engagement to build social license and goodwill.

Contingency Plan: Activate crisis communications protocol; issue statement
within 24 hours; engage with concerned parties directly; correct misinformation.

Residual Risk Rating: 4 - LOW (Green)


CATEGORY E: PROGRAMMATIC RISKS
================================================================================

RISK #12: Beneficiary Targeting Errors
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Programmatic
Description:    Inclusion of non-eligible individuals or exclusion of eligible
                beneficiaries due to targeting errors, elite capture, or
                incomplete vulnerability data, undermining program effectiveness
                and equity.
Likelihood:     3 (Possible)
Impact:         3 (Moderate)
Risk Rating:    9 - MEDIUM (Yellow)
Risk Owner:     Program Manager

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Use validated vulnerability assessment tools (e.g., Household Economy
   Approach, IPC household-level indicators) for beneficiary identification.
2. Community-based targeting with verification by independent committee
   including women, elders, youth, and persons with disabilities.
3. Public display of beneficiary lists (with appropriate data protection)
   for community verification period (7 days) before finalization.
4. Post-distribution monitoring (PDM) with specific questions on targeting
   accuracy, conducted within 30 days of each distribution.
5. Confidential complaints mechanism for reporting targeting concerns.
6. Spot-check verification visits by M&E team to 10% of beneficiary households.

Contingency Plan: Conduct re-verification exercise; replace ineligible
beneficiaries; address elite capture through community dialogue.

Residual Risk Rating: 4 - LOW (Green)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #13: Low Beneficiary Participation or Uptake
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Programmatic
Description:    Target communities do not engage with program activities at
                expected levels due to competing priorities, cultural barriers,
                access constraints, or lack of awareness, resulting in
                underachievement of program targets.
Likelihood:     2 (Unlikely)
Impact:         3 (Moderate)
Risk Rating:    6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)
Risk Owner:     Community Mobilization Officer

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Conduct participatory needs assessments to ensure program design aligns
   with community-expressed priorities.
2. Engage community leaders, religious leaders, and women's groups as program
   champions and mobilizers.
3. Schedule activities at times and locations convenient for target groups,
   with specific attention to women's mobility constraints.
4. Use local language and culturally appropriate messaging in all communications.
5. Provide practical incentives for participation (meals during training,
   childcare support, transport reimbursement).
6. Regular feedback sessions to adapt programming based on community preferences.

Contingency Plan: Conduct rapid assessment to identify barriers; adjust
program design; increase community mobilization resources.

Residual Risk Rating: 3 - LOW (Green)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #14: Climate Shock During Implementation
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Programmatic / Environmental
Description:    Severe drought, flooding, or locusts during the implementation
                period destroys agricultural inputs, displaces beneficiaries,
                and undermines livelihoods recovery activities.
Likelihood:     4 (Likely)
Impact:         4 (Major)
Risk Rating:    16 - CRITICAL (Red)
Risk Owner:     Program Manager

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Integrate climate risk analysis into all agricultural programming using
   seasonal forecasts from SWALIM and FEWS NET.
2. Promote drought-resistant and short-cycle crop varieties.
3. Diversify livelihoods interventions to reduce dependence on rain-fed
   agriculture (livestock, petty trade, vocational skills).
4. Establish community-level early warning systems linked to national platforms.
5. Include contingency budget (5% of agricultural activities budget) for
   replanting or emergency response.
6. Implement soil and water conservation measures at demonstration sites.
7. Pilot crop insurance schemes where feasible.

Contingency Plan: Activate emergency response component; pivot from recovery
to relief activities; request budget reallocation from donor.

Residual Risk Rating: 9 - MEDIUM (Yellow)

-----------------------------------------

RISK #15: Data Quality and Reporting Failures
-----------------------------------------
Category:       Programmatic / Compliance
Description:    Poor data quality, incomplete record-keeping, or delayed
                reporting undermines evidence base for program effectiveness,
                fails to meet donor reporting requirements, and delays
                subsequent disbursements.
Likelihood:     2 (Unlikely)
Impact:         3 (Moderate)
Risk Rating:    6 - MEDIUM (Yellow)
Risk Owner:     M&E Officer

Mitigation Strategies:
1. Implement digital data collection using KoBoToolbox on tablets with
   built-in validation rules to minimize data entry errors.
2. Monthly data quality audits by M&E Officer with random verification of
   10% of records against source documents.
3. Standardized reporting templates with clear deadlines and responsibilities.
4. Quarterly M&E capacity building sessions for program staff on data
   collection, management, and use.
5. Automated data dashboards for real-time monitoring of key indicators.
6. Backup data storage on encrypted cloud server with weekly automated backups.

Contingency Plan: Deploy additional M&E support; request reporting extension
from donor; conduct retroactive data verification exercise.

Residual Risk Rating: 3 - LOW (Green)
